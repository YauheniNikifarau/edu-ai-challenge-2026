# Evently — User Guide

Evently is a platform for creating and attending community events. This guide covers the four main flows: publishing an event, RSVPing as an attendee, accessing your ticket, and checking attendees in at the door.

---

## Flow 1 — Publish an Event

### Step 1 — Create an account

Click **Sign Up** in the top navigation. Enter your email and password, then confirm the verification email sent to your inbox. If you already have an account, click **Sign In** instead.

### Step 2 — Become a host

Before you can create events, you need a host profile. Click **Become a Host** in the navigation menu. Fill in:

- **Host name** — the public name that will appear on your events
- **URL slug** — auto-filled from your name; this becomes your public host page address
- **Logo** — optional, upload an image to represent your organisation
- **Short bio** — a brief description of who you are or what you organise
- **Contact email** — pre-filled from your account, change if needed

Click **Create host**. You will land on your host dashboard.

### Step 3 — Create a new event

From your dashboard, click the **New event** button in the top-right corner. Fill in the event details:

- **Title** — the name of your event
- **Description** — tell attendees what to expect
- **Start date and time / End date and time** — when the event begins and ends
- **Timezone** — select the timezone the times apply to
- **Venue address** — the physical location; leave this blank if the event is online
- **Online link** — a meeting URL for virtual events
- **Capacity** — the maximum number of people who can attend
- **Cover image** — an optional image shown on the event card and detail page
- **Visibility** — choose **Public** so the event appears in the Explore page, or **Unlisted** to share it only via a direct link

Click **Create draft**. The event is saved privately and you are taken to the edit page.

### Step 4 — Publish

On the edit page, review your details and click **Publish**. The event is now live and visible to everyone on the Explore page.

You can click **Unpublish** at any time to hide it again, **Duplicate** to create a copy as a new draft, or **Delete** to remove it permanently.

## Flow 2 — RSVP as an Attendee

### Step 1 — Find an event

Open the **Explore** page from the home screen. Browse the event cards or use the filters at the top to narrow down:

- **Search** — type a keyword from the event title or description
- **Location** — type part of a venue address
- **From / To** — pick a date range using the calendar pickers
- **Include past events** — toggle this on to also show events that have already ended

Each card shows how many people are going and the total capacity.

### Step 2 — Open the event

Click any event card to see the full details: date, time in the event's local timezone, location or online link, and the current attendee count.

### Step 3 — RSVP

Scroll to the bottom of the event page and click **RSVP**. If you are not logged in, you will be taken to the sign-in screen and returned to the event automatically after.

What happens next depends on availability:

- **Spot available** — your RSVP is confirmed instantly and a ticket is generated for you.
- **Event is full** — click **Join Waitlist** to add yourself to the queue. Your position number is shown once you join.
- **Already confirmed** — the button changes to **Cancel RSVP**. Cancelling your spot automatically moves the first person on the waitlist into your place and sends them a notification.
- **On the waitlist** — the button shows your current position and a **Leave waitlist** link if you change your mind.

When you are promoted from the waitlist, a notification badge appears on the bell icon in the top navigation.

## Flow 3 — Your Ticket

### Where to find it

Once your RSVP is confirmed, your ticket appears automatically on two places:

- **On the event page** — scroll past the RSVP button to see the ticket card.
- **My Tickets** — click **My Tickets** in the navigation menu to see all tickets for upcoming events you are attending in one place.

### What the ticket shows

- The event name and date
- Your name as it will appear at check-in
- A **QR code** that staff will scan at the door
- The ticket code in text form, in case a manual entry is needed

### Add the event to your calendar

On the ticket card, click **Add to Google Calendar** to open Google Calendar pre-filled with the event details. Or click **Download .ics** to save a calendar file you can open in Apple Calendar, Outlook, or any other calendar app.

## Flow 4 — Check In Attendees (Host / Staff)

### Who can access the check-in screen

Only users with the **host** or **checker** role can check people in. To add a team member, go to your dashboard, scroll to the **Team** section, enter their email address, and send an invite. They follow the link in the email to accept and gain access.

### Opening the check-in screen

From your dashboard, find the event and click the **Check-in** button on its card. A dedicated check-in screen opens showing live counters:

- **Checked in / Confirmed** — how many confirmed attendees have arrived
- **Waitlist** — how many people are still waiting for a spot

These numbers update automatically in real time as your team checks people in.

### Checking someone in

Ask the attendee to show their QR code or ticket code. Either scan the QR code with a barcode scanner (it types the code directly into the field) or paste / type the code manually into the **Ticket code** field. Press **Check In**.

You will see a confirmation toast with the attendee's name. The input clears automatically so you are ready for the next person.

If a code has already been used, the screen shows an **Already checked in** warning. If the code is not found, it shows **Ticket not found**.

### Undoing a check-in

If you check in the wrong person, click **Undo last check-in** to reverse it. This works for the most recent check-in recorded in your current session.

## Other Things You Can Do

**Export attendees** — on any event card in your dashboard, click **Export CSV** to download a spreadsheet listing all confirmed attendees with their RSVP and check-in status.

**Event gallery** — after RSVPing, confirmed attendees can upload photos to the event's gallery directly from the event page. As a host, you can approve or reject submitted photos from the Past Events section of your dashboard.

**Post-event feedback** — once your event has ended, confirmed attendees see a feedback form on the event page. Their responses are collected for your reference.

**Report content** — if you spot a problem with an event, scroll to the bottom of the event page and click **Report** to flag it for review.
